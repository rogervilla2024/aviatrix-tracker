Understanding the Basics of Aviatrix

At its core, Aviatrix follows the familiar crash game format. An aircraft takes off, and a multiplier begins to increase from 1.00x. Players must decide when to cash out before the aircraft flies away (crashes), taking any unclaimed winnings with it.

The fundamental gameplay loop is straightforward:

  1. Place your bet before the round begins
  2. Watch the aircraft take off as the multiplier increases
  3. Cash out at any time to secure your winnings
  4. If you don't cash out before the crash, you lose your bet

How the Multiplier Works

The multiplier in Aviatrix starts at 1.00x when the aircraft takes off and increases rapidly. The rate of increase and the final crash point are determined by a random algorithm before each round begins.

Important points about the multiplier:

  • Higher multipliers are statistically less likely but possible

Understanding the Gambler's Fallacy

Many players believe that after several low multiplier rounds, a high multiplier is "due." This is a common misconception. Each round in Aviatrix is completely independent, and past results have absolutely no influence on future outcomes. The random number generator treats each round as a fresh start.
