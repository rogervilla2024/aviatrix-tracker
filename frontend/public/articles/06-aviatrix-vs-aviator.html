Core Gameplay Comparison

At their core, both Aviatrix and Aviator follow the same basic crash game formula:

  1. Place a bet before the round begins
  2. Watch an aircraft take off as the multiplier increases
  3. Cash out before the crash to win
  4. If you don't cash out in time, you lose your bet

The fundamental mechanics are nearly identical, making both games easy to understand for anyone familiar with crash games.

Key Differences Explained

1. NFT Aircraft System

Aviatrix's biggest differentiator is its NFT aircraft collection system. This feature allows players to:

  • Own unique NFT aircraft as digital assets
  • Customize their in-game aircraft appearance
  • Potentially receive special benefits from rare aircraft
  • Trade or sell aircraft to other players

Aviator does not have any NFT integration. It focuses purely on the crash game mechanics without additional collectible features.

NFT Consideration

The NFT system in Aviatrix is optional - you can play the base game without owning any NFTs. However, if you're interested in digital collectibles and blockchain gaming, Aviatrix offers this additional layer of engagement that Aviator lacks.

2. Developer and History

Aviator by Spribe launched in 2019 and quickly became one of the most popular crash games worldwide. Spribe is an established gaming provider with multiple successful titles.

Aviatrix by Aviatrix.bet is a newer entrant to the market, positioning itself as an innovative alternative with NFT features. It aims to differentiate through blockchain integration and unique digital asset ownership.

3. Market Presence

Aviator has a significant head start in terms of availability:

  • Available at hundreds of online casinos worldwide
  • Established player base and community
  • Proven track record over several years

Aviatrix is still growing its presence:

  • Available at select platforms
  • Building community through NFT ecosystem
  • Newer but innovative approach

4. Visual Design

Both games feature aviation themes, but with different aesthetics:

  • Aviator: Simple, clean interface with a red aircraft. Minimalist design focused on functionality.
  • Aviatrix: More elaborate visuals with various NFT aircraft designs. Customizable experience based on owned NFTs.

RTP and Odds: Are They Equal?

Yes, both games offer 97% RTP and a 3% house edge. In terms of pure mathematical odds, neither game has an advantage over the other.

Important Understanding

The same RTP means that over time, both games will return approximately the same percentage to players. Neither game is "better" for winning - both have the same house edge. The differences are in features, aesthetics, and additional systems like NFTs, not in odds.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the main difference between Aviatrix and Aviator?
The main difference is that Aviatrix features an NFT aircraft collection system, allowing players to own and customize unique digital aircraft. Aviator by Spribe has traditional gameplay without NFT integration. Both games have similar core crash mechanics and 97% RTP.
Which game has better RTP - Aviatrix or Aviator?
Both Aviatrix and Aviator have the same RTP of 97%, meaning both return approximately $97 for every $100 wagered over time. The house edge is 3% in both games.
Should I play Aviatrix or Aviator?
Both games have similar mechanics and RTP. Choose Aviatrix if you're interested in NFT features and aircraft customization. Choose Aviator if you prefer simpler gameplay from an established provider. Remember that both are gambling games with house edges.
Can I use Aviator strategies in Aviatrix?
The games share similar mechanics, so general approaches (like bankroll management) apply to both. However, no "strategy" can beat the house edge in either game. Each game's rounds are independent and random.
Is one game more fair than the other?
Both games use provably fair systems to ensure random, verifiable outcomes. Neither is "more fair" - both operate with transparent, auditable randomness and the same 97% RTP.
