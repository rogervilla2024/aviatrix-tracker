The Reality of "Patterns"

Many players claim to see patterns in Aviatrix flight data. Common beliefs include:

  • "After several low crashes, a high multiplier is due"
  • "The game follows predictable cycles"
  • "Certain times of day have better multipliers"
  • "If you watch enough rounds, you can predict the next one"

The Gambler's Fallacy

All of these beliefs are false. This is the gambler's fallacy - the mistaken belief that past random events affect future random events. In reality:

  • Each round uses an independent random number generator
  • The RNG has no memory of previous rounds
  • Ten crashes at 1.1x does NOT make 10x more likely next
  • Human brains are wired to see patterns, even in pure randomness

Variance and Streaks

Even with random outcomes, streaks and clusters naturally occur. Understanding this helps prevent emotional decision-making:

Natural Streak Probabilities

  • 3 consecutive crashes below 2x: ~13.7% chance (happens regularly)
  • 5 consecutive crashes below 2x: ~3.6% chance (still common)
  • 10 consecutive crashes below 2x: ~0.13% chance (rare but happens)
  • 2 consecutive 10x+ multipliers: ~0.94% chance (very rare)

These streaks are completely normal in random systems and do NOT indicate any pattern or future outcome.

How Aviatrix Generates Random Outcomes

Aviatrix uses a provably fair system to generate crash points. The typical process involves:

  1. Server Seed: A cryptographic seed generated before each round
  2. Client Seed: Input from players or public data
  3. Nonce: A counter that increments each round
  4. Hashing: These values are combined using SHA-256 or similar algorithms
  5. Crash Point: The hash result determines the multiplier

This system ensures that outcomes are predetermined before bets are placed and can be verified after each round.

What Statistics Cannot Tell You
